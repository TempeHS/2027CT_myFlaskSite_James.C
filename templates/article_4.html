Learning Basic Street Art Styles

Posted on June 12, 2026 | By Admin

Street art is a diverse and constantly evolving form of creative expression. Before developing your own unique style, it helps to understand the basic techniques and approaches that many artists use. Each style has its own history, tools, and visual impact.

Whether you're interested in bold graffiti lettering, detailed stencil work, or simple wheat-paste posters, learning the fundamentals will give you a strong foundation to build from.

Graffiti Writing (Tagging and Letter Styles)

One of the most recognised forms of street art is graffiti writing. This style focuses on stylised lettering, often used to represent an artist’s name or alias. It ranges from simple tags to highly complex “wildstyle” lettering with overlapping shapes and arrows.

Beginners often start with tags, which are quick signatures done with markers or spray paint. Over time, artists develop more advanced lettering styles by experimenting with shape, flow, and balance.

A key part of graffiti writing is consistency. Practising the same letters repeatedly helps build muscle memory and improves control, especially when working quickly on walls or public surfaces.

Stencil Art

Stencil art is a popular street art technique that involves cutting shapes or designs into a material like cardboard or plastic, then spraying paint over it to create a repeated image.

This method allows for sharp, clean designs and can be reproduced quickly across multiple locations. Because of this, stencil art is often used for symbols, portraits, or politically themed imagery.

Beginners can start by designing simple shapes and gradually move toward more detailed multi-layer stencils. Each layer adds depth and colour to the final piece.

Wheat-Pasting Posters

Wheat-pasting is another widely used technique in street art. It involves creating printed or hand-drawn artwork on paper and sticking it to walls using a simple adhesive mixture.

This style is often used for posters, illustrations, or repeated graphic designs. It allows artists to prepare their work in advance and install it quickly in public spaces.

Wheat-paste art is especially effective for storytelling, as it can be used to display series of images or messages across multiple locations.

Sticker Art (Slaps)

Sticker art, also known as “slaps,” is one of the easiest entry points into street art. Artists design small graphics or logos, print them as stickers, and place them in public spaces.

This style is fast, portable, and highly repeatable, making it popular among beginners and experienced artists alike. Despite its simplicity, sticker art can still have a strong visual impact when used creatively.

Developing Your Own Style

Once you understand the basics, the next step is experimentation. Most street artists combine elements from multiple styles to create something unique.

Don’t focus too much on perfection at the start. Instead, focus on consistency, practice, and exploring different techniques. Over time, your personal style will naturally develop.

Street art is not just about technique—it’s about expression. The more you create, the more your work will reflect your own identity and perspective.
