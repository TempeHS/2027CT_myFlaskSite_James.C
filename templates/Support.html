Support & FAQ

Got questions about street art or graffiti? Find answers to common questions below.

General Questions

What is graffiti?

Graffiti is a form of visual expression usually created in public spaces using spray paint, markers, or other tools. It can range from simple tags to detailed artwork and murals.

Is graffiti legal?

Graffiti is only legal when it is done with permission from the property owner or as part of a commissioned project. Unauthorized graffiti on public or private property is illegal in most places.

What’s the difference between graffiti and street art?

Graffiti often focuses on lettering, tags, and stylised names, while street art includes a wider range of styles such as murals, stencils, posters, and installations. Street art is generally more visual and image-based.

Learning & Style Questions

How do beginners start learning graffiti?

Most beginners start with simple hand styles and tagging using pen or marker. Practising letter structure and consistency is the first step before moving on to spray paint.

Do I need expensive tools to start?

No. Many artists begin with basic markers, sketchbooks, or digital drawing apps. Spray paint and advanced tools come later as skills develop.

How long does it take to get good?

It depends on practice. Like any art form, consistent drawing and experimenting with styles will improve your skills over time.

Safety & Community

Where can I legally practice graffiti?

Many cities have designated legal walls or art spaces where graffiti is allowed. Community art programs and festivals also provide safe opportunities to paint legally.

How can I respect other artists?

Respecting existing artwork, avoiding covering others’ pieces without permission, and following local rules are important parts of graffiti culture.

Is graffiti part of street art culture?

Yes. Graffiti is one of the foundational elements of street art culture and has heavily influenced modern urban art worldwide.
